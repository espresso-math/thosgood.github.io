Torsors and principal bundles

In my thesis, switching between vector bundles and principal -bundles has often made certain problems easier (or harder) to understand. Due to my innate fear of all things differentially geometric, I often prefer working with principal bundles, and since reading Stephen Sontz’s (absolutely fantastic) book Principal Bundles — The Classical Case, I’ve really grown quite fond of bundles, especially when you start talking about all the lovely and things therein12. Point is, I haven’t posted anything in forever, and one of my supervisor’s strong pedagogical beliefs is that ‘affine vector spaces should be understood as -torsors, where is the underlying vector space acting via translation’,3 which makes a nice short topic of discussion, whence this post.

We briefly4 recall the definition of a principal -bundle over a space , where is some topological group.

Definition. A principal -bundle over is a fibre bundle with a continuous right action such that
